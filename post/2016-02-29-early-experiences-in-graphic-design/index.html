Early Experiences in Graphic Design

Feb 29, 2016 6 min read

This time I’m going to talk about a kind of spin-off of my previous post, where I explained some experiences in the PSP scene. The parallel experience consisted in learning how to use design programs like Adobe Photoshop and GIMP. Here is the explanation of how I started designing signatures in forums!

As soon as I had my PSP hacked, my participation in forums began to increase. I had previously visited some of them but just as a visitor, especially in ESPALPSP as I explained in the first post related to these “adventures”. However, although it might seem strange, I didn’t start to post in the PSP related sections, but in the graphic design one!

The very first thing I remember when entering in the design section was a post about a tournament… but about what? It was about signature design. By that time I had no idea what a signature was. The signature is what always appears when you post something in a forum; users usually put an image instead of text. I was completely determined to participate even though I had never used GIMP or Photoshop.

The topic of the tournament was about a creature of the manga Bleach whose name was Kon. Being highly motivated to achieve something, I did some “research” for tutorials about getting started with this new software. I don’t remember if I spent many days with the design, but I definitely got something, which is shown below. I knew it was simple, the characters were not very well mixed with the background, and there were too many effects applied, but I felt (and I feel) very proud of it.

You might have wondered what does M14_b2 mean? Well, that was my second name in the Internet after Ertsiger (which I have recently started to use again). Actually, I borrowed it from my mother. She created it by using her initials, ’M’ (name) and ‘B’ (surname), followed by their respective numbers in the alphabet (14 and 2). Sometime later I searched ‘M14’ and ‘b2’ in Google separately, and they turned out to be (unfortunately) a rifle and a bomber!

Returning to the contest… I didn’t won. There were very talented people who made amazing stuff! However, I received some votes and feedback that made me feel extremely happy. I really felt it was a very rewarding experience, so I kept on experimenting and participating in signature designing contests for more than 4 years (always in PSP related forums).

By joining these tournaments at ESPALPSP I started to establish good relationships with some of the members that participated the most. I remember the name of three who I really admired: Ozzblf, Hirakun, and JDesign. I specially have huge appreciation for Ozzblf, he designed me two avatars that I used during all my life in the PSP scene, and also collaborated with me in my first PSP program with graphics (NeoHider, which I mention in the previous post).

  • The first one was rounded avatar which had an image of Kakashi Hatake from the manga Naruto; although I had never read or watched Naruto, it was extremely popular at that time (a lot of people had avatars related to it), so I wanted one. I asked him to do this avatar through a post which he used as a personal workshop (some designers used to have one).

  • The second one was a squared avatar which contained the text M14_b2 in a very original layout. He told me that it was inspired by cranes that were used to make a new building in front of his house. I loved it, I really appreciated his efforts! Besides, he told me that he was very happy because of trusting him so much, which even increased my respect for him and his work.

ESPALPSP was not the only place where I participated. Sometime after I started posting at ESPALPSP, another similar website called EstrenosPSP launched. Given my huge interest in designing, I went there looking forward to more challenges. However, the activity there was not very high by that time, so I decided to take charge of the “Graphic Design” section and ask the admins to become a moderator. The person who attracted me to this website (his name was The_ugly and had Eli Wallach as his avatar, although he after changed his name to Wizard_PsP or something similar) knew about my interest and quickly named me moderator.

I felt incredibly happy, so I organized the first weekly tournament of signature design after some time; however, we were not as many people (3 or 4) as in ESPALPSP (usually +20), so it was not as competitive. Unfortunately, the website did not last too much, so I had to visit other websites. Furthermore, I was banned in ESPALPSP because of private messages publicizing EstrenosPSP, i.e. spam (enough good reasons to do so).

I continued designing signatures at two websites: PSPStation.org (now PSNeo.com) and TecMAX.net. The first one is the forum in which I have participated the most (it was mainly about PSP, but now is about everything related to consoles); I stopped posting there in 2010. TecMAX.net was a website that I launched together with three Internet colleagues: GERMAX, The_dark and valen_9. In both forums I used to organize and participate in designing challenges, and I even won my first competitions there. Without a doubt, I consider this time the best as a designer (if I can consider myself as such, since the only thing I usually did was modifying already existing things). I had even received some “orders”, which made me feel very well, especially when people were happy about the results!

Finally, I want to say that I didn’t only designed signatures and avatars, but also interfaces (for PSP applications, of course)! I did interfaces for my applications and also for the programming team in which I was involved. Apart from these ones, I also did it for other people. Concretely, there was a coder called Recse who made an app named iApplicationX, and he asked me to do the interface for him. As he seemed friendly to me, I accepted (he told me to use a wallpaper he found, and make the design over it).

And that’s it! I hope you have liked this new post. If you want to start doing this kind of stuff, I highly encourage you to visit DeviantArt. Extremely talented designers post their works there (lots of them done from scratch, which is even more praiseworthy). I used to post some of my work there. In fact, after writing this post, I have noted that lots of things I have done are not there. I believe someday I will make an update. You can check my gallery out by clicking here.

See you soon!
